1. The Hard Facts: What to Consider

Switching to Linux is great for privacy, speed, and customization. However, let's look at the reality before you jump:

Category The Reality on Linux
MS Office Native apps do NOT work. You must use web versions (Office 365) or alternatives like LibreOffice or ONLYOFFICE.
Adobe Creative Cloud Photoshop/Premiere do NOT work natively. Alternatives: DaVinci Resolve (Video), GIMP/Krita (Photo), Figma (UI).
Gaming EXCELLENT via Steam Proton. Most Windows games run perfectly. Exception: Games with Kernel-level Anti-Cheat (like Valorant or Fortnite) will not work.
Hardware Support Generally plug-and-play. Printers and older hardware often work better on Linux than on Windows 11.

3. Your First Meaningful Steps

  • Step 1: Back Up Everything. Put your important files on an external hard drive or in the cloud. Formatting drives deletes everything!
  • Step 2: Get a USB Stick (8GB+). Download your chosen Linux ISO file and use a tool like BalenaEtcher or Rufus to flash it onto the USB stick.
  • Step 3: Try the "Live Environment". You don't have to install Linux right away. Boot from the USB stick and select "Try Linux". It runs from the USB so you can test your Wi-Fi, audio, and the interface without touching your hard drive.
  • Step 4: Install. Once you are ready, click the "Install" icon on the Live Desktop and follow the simple on-screen instructions.
